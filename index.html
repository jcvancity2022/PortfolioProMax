BOUWHULPGROEP: INTERACTIVE WALL FOR LOW-INCOME HOMEOWNERS

UX/UI — Scalable Design, TU/E

An interactive wall designed to engage homeowners in sustainable renovations. This hands-on tool integrates tangible elements, allowing residents to explore renovation benefits in an accessible and interactive way.

Project Overview

An interactive folding wall designed to engage low-income and first-generation migrant homeowners in Woensel West, Netherlands, by making sustainable renovation benefits (heat, health, financial savings) tangible and accessible. Developed as part of the Transforming Practices (DEP004) course at Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e) in collaboration with Bouwhulpgroep.

The design aimed to:

  • Simplify complex technical renovation information.
  • Promote community involvement and trust.
  • Be inclusive for residents with diverse literacy and cultural backgrounds.
  • Provide developer-ready UX specifications transferable to digital platforms for scalability.

Key Skills Applied

UX & Interaction Design
Created engagement-focused layouts, ergonomic interaction elements, and detailed UX specifications.
Design Systems Thinking
Built modular folding wall components as scalable patterns, aligning with enterprise-level design systems.
User Research & Field Studies
Conducted on-site observations and analysis of socio-economic/cultural barriers.
Accessibility & Inclusivity
Applied WCAG-informed clarity and cultural sensitivity to support broad community use.
Communication & Visualization
Produced story maps, impact diagrams, and stakeholder presentations.

Research & Insights

A community-led initiative promoted environmental awareness through green spaces, encouraging neighbourhood participation.
  • Conducted on-site walkthroughs and contextual research in Woensel West to understand renovation barriers.
  • Found residents were often hesitant to engage directly due to low trust and legal/consent constraints.
  • This shaped the solution toward passive, experiential interaction, ensuring the wall could communicate benefits independently.
Site visit to Woensel West to analyze neighbourhood dynamics and renovation challenges.
The interactive board prototype mapped the social, financial, and aesthetic impacts of renovations.

Design Constraints

Due to legal and ethical requirements, we could not conduct in-depth homeowner testing without formal consent. This shaped our design toward passive-experiential methods and a standalone solution that communicated benefits clearly without facilitation.

One of the project's challenges was securing homeowner participation due to consent form requirements.

Initial Concepts & Prototypes

Tested early ideas such as feedback boards and renovation benefit maps, then built successive prototypes focused on tangible, engaging storytelling:

  • Roof insulation models → lacked engagement.
  • Ergonomic mouse-shaped handle → encouraged intuitive interaction.
  • Tactile representations (heat pad, health symbol, coin imagery) → made abstract benefits concrete.
Problem

Initial roof insulation prototype lacked engagement and failed to clearly communicate renovation benefits to homeowners.

Solution

Redesigned into an interactive folding wall with tangible embedded objects making abstract concepts concrete and intuitive.

Prototyping & Iteration

The final wall consisted of three folding panels — each revealing a renovation benefit: heat retention, health, and financial savings — with embedded tangible objects and an ergonomic handle. This modular structure was documented in developer-ready UX specifications showing how patterns could scale into web-based or enterprise design systems.

Reflection & Learnings

  • Adapt methods under legal and cultural constraints.
  • Prioritize experiential design and accessibility over technical precision.
  • Communicate effectively through verbal presentations, visual documentation, and developer-ready specs.
  • Apply design system thinking to ensure modularity, consistency, and scalability across digital and physical contexts.

By combining tangible interaction, environmental storytelling, and scalable design system logic, our interactive wall transformed abstract renovation concepts into intuitive, user-centred experiences — empowering residents while meeting enterprise-level design standards.

TALESHUB: SENIOR SOCIAL CONNECTIVITY APP

UX/UI — Accessible Storytelling for Seniors

Digital prototype created by Jeffrey Cheng, Howard Chang, Han Yan using Figma and Protopie. TalesHub — an app designed to reduce social isolation among seniors through storytelling and meaningful connection. Click the image above to view the live prototype.

Project Description

Developed in IAT 334: Interface Design at Simon Fraser University, TalesHub was a team project designing an app specifically for seniors aged 70+ living alone, focusing on emotional connection, accessibility, and trust. The design needed to provide an intuitive and inclusive interface for older adults with varying digital literacy, simplify onboarding, ensure privacy and consent controls, and offer developer-ready UX specifications.

Key Skills Applied

UX & Interaction Design
Wireframing, user flows, onboarding design, and accessibility-focused refinements.
Digital Prototyping
Built interactive mid- and high-fidelity prototypes using Figma and ProtoPie.
User Research & Testing
Conducted interviews and usability testing with seniors to identify pain points.
Accessibility & Inclusivity
Applied clear labels, tooltips, and WCAG-aligned design principles.
Privacy & Security Design
Integrated consent-based settings and onboarding customization to empower users.

Research & Ideation

Key insights from interviews and secondary research: loneliness and disconnection are common among seniors living alone; storytelling is a powerful method of emotional bonding; existing social apps are often unintuitive and inaccessible for older users. These insights drove our goal to design a platform that was simple, supportive, and trust-building.

Research conducted among seniors gathering data of 70+ year old adults living alone who need emotional and social connection.

Digital Prototyping & Testing

We created a mid-fidelity prototype in Figma and built interactive flows in ProtoPie for usability testing, featuring a simplified Story Hub, redesigned step-by-step onboarding, and contextual tooltips alongside icons.

Prototype showing the steps to navigate the TalesHub App for seniors.

Navigation Issues — Before & After

Before

Vague terms, unclear icons, minimalist layouts causing confusion and frustration for older users.

After

Added contextual tooltips, text labels alongside icons, navigation shortcuts, and improved button contrast and visibility.

Before: unclear navigation patterns
After: improved labels and guided onboarding

Privacy & Security — Before & After

During testing, users voiced concerns about location visibility in features like "Near Me" and the lack of consent-based controls such as blocking and visibility settings. These caused hesitation, as seniors wanted more control over who could see their activity.
Before

During testing, users voiced concerns about: Location visibility in features like “Near Me” Lack of consent-based controls (e.g., blocking, visibility settings) These two settings caused hesitation, as seniors wanted more control over who could see their activity.

After

To address these concerns, we redesigned the onboarding to include: Bio and profile photo setup Visibility toggles and privacy preferences Customizable sharing settings from the start The updated onboarding built trust, increased engagement confidence, and made the platform feel safer and more personal. Seniors felt more comfortable exploring features knowing they had control over their visibility and interactions.

Users wanted more control over privacy. Lack of consent-based features caused hesitation with "Near Me."
Onboarding updates now let users set up their bio, upload a photo, and adjust privacy from the start — building trust and confidence.

Reflection

  • Simpler language and guided onboarding are crucial for comfort and clarity.
  • Thoughtful privacy controls empower users and reduce friction.
  • Small tweaks to layout and labels can dramatically increase usability.

TalesHub ultimately offers seniors a safe, intuitive space to share stories, connect with others, and feel emotionally supported — without compromising clarity or comfort.

BARONAV: SCALABLE LOCATION-BASED BAROMETRIC PRESSURE APP

UX/UI — Real-Time Data UX & Scalable Design

A mobile app that leverages smartphone barometric sensors and GPS to provide real-time, location-based weather insights. BaroNav transforms raw atmospheric data into intuitive, actionable forecasts within a clean, responsive interface.

Project Description

Developed as the final group project for Mobile Computing, BaroNav integrates custom sensor processing, interactive maps, and external weather APIs. My role spanned mobile development and UX/UI design, ensuring the app balanced technical performance with user-centred clarity.

Main Functionalities & Development

Mobile Development
Built Android features in Java/XML with Android Studio.
API Integration
Created a custom WeatherService for efficient data binding and real-time updates.
UX & Interaction Design
Wireframes, prototypes, and refined layouts in Figma.
UI Development
Responsive XML layouts for maps, data panels, and navigation.
Accessibility & Responsiveness
Improved layout hierarchy, button visibility, and consistency.
Agile Collaboration
Refined features through peer feedback in iterative Agile cycles.
Essential elements for the home page and weather data — designed in Figma and developed in XML, Java and Android Studio.

Challenge 1: Weather Data Integration

Problem

High-volume API data risked slowing down the UI. Users could not see weather updates instantly or clearly.

Solution

Built a custom WeatherService with OkHttpClient to fetch, parse, and bind data in real time — ensuring smooth performance and responsive weather updates.

Data obtained to display weather conditions.
Data displayed on the home page after retrieval.

Challenge 2: Map Navigation Complexity

Problem

Early map prototypes made it difficult to add, edit, and manage multiple saved locations. Layout lacked hierarchy, making navigation unintuitive.

Solution

Redesigned Add/Edit Location components with pin-based selection and manual entry. Polished navigation flow through iterative testing — resulting in seamless, accessible location management.

Edit location UI
XML code for Add Location activity
XML code for Edit Location activity

Reflection

  • Mobile development with Java, XML, and Android Studio.
  • Sensor integration (barometer) and real-time data handling.
  • API integration and dynamic UI data binding.
  • Designing for clarity, accessibility, and responsive layouts.
  • Iterative Agile teamwork and refining based on user feedback.

BaroNav demonstrates how structured UX design and clear system logic can transform complex sensor data into simple, actionable insights — bridging technical constraints with user-centred clarity.
