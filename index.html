BOUWHULPGROEP: INTERACTIVE WALL FOR LOW-INCOME HOMEOWNERS

UX/UI — Scalable Design, TU/E

An interactive wall designed to engage homeowners in sustainable renovations. This hands-on tool integrates tangible elements, allowing residents to explore renovation benefits in an accessible and interactive way.

Project Overview

An interactive folding wall designed to engage low-income and first-generation migrant homeowners in Woensel West, Netherlands, by making sustainable renovation benefits (heat, health, financial savings) tangible and accessible. Developed as part of the Transforming Practices (DEP004) course at Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e) in collaboration with Bouwhulpgroep.

The design aimed to:

  • Simplify complex technical renovation information.
  • Promote community involvement and trust.
  • Be inclusive for residents with diverse literacy and cultural backgrounds.
  • Provide developer-ready UX specifications transferable to digital platforms for scalability.

Key Skills Applied

UX & Interaction Design
Created engagement-focused layouts, ergonomic interaction elements, and detailed UX specifications.
Design Systems Thinking
Built modular folding wall components as scalable patterns, aligning with enterprise-level design systems.
User Research & Field Studies
Conducted on-site observations and analysis of socio-economic/cultural barriers.
Accessibility & Inclusivity
Applied WCAG-informed clarity and cultural sensitivity to support broad community use.
Communication & Visualization
Produced story maps, impact diagrams, and stakeholder presentations.

Research & Insights

A community-led initiative promoted environmental awareness through green spaces, encouraging neighbourhood participation.
  • Conducted on-site walkthroughs and contextual research in Woensel West to understand renovation barriers.
  • Found residents were often hesitant to engage directly due to low trust and legal/consent constraints.
  • This shaped the solution toward passive, experiential interaction, ensuring the wall could communicate benefits independently.
Site visit to Woensel West to analyze neighbourhood dynamics and renovation challenges.
The interactive board prototype mapped the social, financial, and aesthetic impacts of renovations.

Design Constraints

Due to legal and ethical requirements, we could not conduct in-depth homeowner testing without formal consent. This shaped our design toward passive-experiential methods and a standalone solution that communicated benefits clearly without facilitation.

One of the project's challenges was securing homeowner participation due to consent form requirements.

Initial Concepts & Prototypes

Tested early ideas such as feedback boards and renovation benefit maps, then built successive prototypes focused on tangible, engaging storytelling:

  • Roof insulation models → lacked engagement.
  • Ergonomic mouse-shaped handle → encouraged intuitive interaction.
  • Tactile representations (heat pad, health symbol, coin imagery) → made abstract benefits concrete.
Problem

Initial roof insulation prototype lacked engagement and failed to clearly communicate renovation benefits to homeowners.

Solution

Redesigned into an interactive folding wall with tangible embedded objects making abstract concepts concrete and intuitive.

Prototyping & Iteration

The final wall consisted of three folding panels — each revealing a renovation benefit: heat retention, health, and financial savings — with embedded tangible objects and an ergonomic handle. This modular structure was documented in developer-ready UX specifications showing how patterns could scale into web-based or enterprise design systems.

Reflection & Learnings

  • Adapt methods under legal and cultural constraints.
  • Prioritize experiential design and accessibility over technical precision.
  • Communicate effectively through verbal presentations, visual documentation, and developer-ready specs.
  • Apply design system thinking to ensure modularity, consistency, and scalability across digital and physical contexts.

By combining tangible interaction, environmental storytelling, and scalable design system logic, our interactive wall transformed abstract renovation concepts into intuitive, user-centred experiences — empowering residents while meeting enterprise-level design standards.

TALESHUB: Senior Social Connectivity App

Accessible Storytelling for Seniors

A digital app designed to reduce social isolation among seniors through storytelling and meaningful connection. Click on the image to see the website of the TalesHub App.

Problem

Seniors living alone often experience social isolation and struggle to navigate complex or unintuitive digital platforms.

Existing apps lacked clarity, accessibility, and trust-building features.

Goal

Design an intuitive and inclusive platform that enables seniors to connect through storytelling while ensuring accessibility, clarity, and trust.

Key Insight

Seniors value clarity, guidance, and control.
Interfaces must prioritize simple language, visible actions, and trust-building interactions.

Research & Insights

  • Seniors living alone frequently experience loneliness and disconnection
  • Storytelling is a natural and effective way to build emotional connection
  • Many existing apps are difficult to navigate due to unclear labels and hidden actions

Research is conducted among seniors gathering the data of 70 year old adults who are living alone who needs emotion and social connection.

Solution

Designed a storytelling platform with simplified onboarding, clear labels, and privacy controls to support safe, meaningful interaction.

This is the prototype showing the steps to navigate the TalesHub App for seniors.

Iteration

Before

Unclear terminology and minimal layouts caused confusion
Users struggled with navigation and understanding actions

After

Introduced tooltips, clear labels, and navigation shortcuts
Improved button visibility and interaction clarity
Result: smoother task flow and reduced user frustration

Testing showed older users were confused by vague terms and unclear icons. Minimal layouts lacked clarity, leading to frustration. Clearer labels and guided onboarding were needed.

Updated Figma screens demonstrate added tooltips, improved labels, and shortcut buttons—making navigation faster and more intuitive for older adults.

Privacy & Trust (Before → After)

Before

Users were concerned about location visibility and lack of control
Features like “Near Me” created hesitation

After

Added privacy toggles, profile setup, and visibility controls during onboarding
Result: increased trust and confidence in using the platform

Users wanted more control over privacy. Lack of consent-based features (like toggles and blocking) caused hesitation with features such as “Near Me.”
Onboarding updates now let users set up their bio, upload a photo, and adjust privacy from the start—building trust and user confidence.

Design Decisions

  • Simplified onboarding with step-by-step guidance
  • Added labels and tooltips to support clarity
  • Introduced navigation shortcuts for key actions
  • Integrated privacy-first interaction design

Outcome

  • Improved usability and accessibility for older users
  • Reduced confusion and increased engagement confidence
  • Created a safe, intuitive environment for storytelling and connection

Reflection

  • Clear language and guided interaction are essential for accessibility
  • Trust and privacy directly impact user engagement
  • Small interface improvements can significantly enhance usability

BARONAV: Location-Based Weather App

Real-Time Data UX & Scalable Design

A mobile app that transforms sensor and API data into clear, real-time weather insights.

Problem

Raw weather and sensor data were complex and difficult for users to interpret.

Map navigation for managing locations was unintuitive and lacked clear structure.

Goal

Design a responsive mobile experience that presents real-time weather data clearly while enabling intuitive location management.

Built using Figma, Android (XML, Java) with a custom WeatherService for real-time data handling.

Key Insight

Users struggled to interpret raw sensor data in real time, making it difficult to understand current conditions or act quickly.

→ The interface needed to translate complex data into clear, prioritized insights.

Solution

Designed a mobile experience that translates real-time sensor and API data into clear, structured weather insights, enabling users to quickly understand conditions and manage locations intuitively.

The data is being displayed on the home page after the data is obtained.

Iteration

Iteration 1

Basic data display & early map layout

Cluttered interface made key information hard to scan and interpret

Iteration 2

Improved hierarchy and structured data panels

Better clarity, but navigation remained complex

Final Design

Refined UI + location management system

Clear data, smooth interaction, intuitive navigation

Early iterations focused on improving location management, enabling users to easily add, edit, and organize saved locations.

Design Decisions

  • Structured data hierarchy → reduced cognitive load when interpreting weather data
  • Custom WeatherService → ensured fast, reliable real-time updates
  • Flexible location input (pin + manual) → supported different user behaviours
  • Consistent layout system → improved usability across screens

The XML code was modified to make the desired appearance with add and edit location option.

Constraints

High-volume API and sensor data required efficient processing to avoid UI lag.

The design balanced performance with real-time responsiveness.

Historical weather data table used to display sensor readings over time.

Outcome

Improved clarity and usability of weather data, enabling faster and more intuitive decision-making.

Achieved smooth real-time updates with a scalable system architecture.

Final UI demonstrating improved data clarity and streamlined location management.

Reflection on Solutions

  • Balanced technical constraints with user-centred design
  • Improved ability to simplify complex data into clear interfaces
  • Strengthened collaboration between development and UX design
